Introduction

Posted on 27 July 2023 (can be any description)

Have you heard the rumor? It's school mystery number one-The Three Clock keepers. There's a big, big old clock somewhere in the school. That clock is time itself-the time that flows through the school. If you move the hands of that clock, the school's time is yours to command. But you see.....There are guards watching over the big clock. They're the three clock keepers who represent past, present, and future. If anyone touches the clock without their permission...The three clock keepers will steal the time from that person's life!

The three clock keepers are the first of the Seven Mysteries in a manga known as 'Jibaku no Shounen Hanako-kun'. It is the fourth school mystery to be introduced in the series. They are the first school mystery to be seen independently outside of their boundary and unrelated to their rumor. The clock keepers are those who oversees the time aspect of Kamome Academy, a school, the are able to control time and hold power because of the Big Clock at the Sealed Auditorium, which is only opened during school festivals. If the Clock were to be destroyed, time would be paused, the clock keepers boundary would open, and the person who broke the clock is able to control the time. We are able to see an example of this in chapter 107 of the manga. Glossary

Words meant to be defined are placed here.

Mirai. in English, Mirai means"future". Kako. in English, Kako means"past". Mirai and Kako don't actually have names, so when Akane first joined the Clock Keepers these were the names he gave the two of them. The reason why Akane had named them Mirai and Kako is because Mirai controls the future, whereas Kako controls the past.
